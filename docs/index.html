How this works

What is Polymarket?

A prediction market where people bet on real-world events. Each market has a YES and NO price between $0 and $1. If YES wins, YES-shares pay $1. The current price is the crowd's estimated probability — YES at $0.45 means the crowd thinks there's a 45% chance.

How to make money

Buy YES when you think the probability is higher than the market price. Buy NO when you think it's lower. Your edge = your estimate − the market.

The three labels

🟢 Buy Candidate Liquid market, fair price range, time to play out. Worth your analysis.

🟡 Watch Either thin liquidity, crowded price, or weird timing. Possible but risky.

🔴 Skip Near-certain outcome or basically no liquidity. The math rarely works.

The 5 rules

  1. Only bet on what you understand. Edge comes from knowledge, not gut feel.
  2. Write down your probability BEFORE looking at the market price. Otherwise you'll anchor.
  3. If your estimate is within 5% of the market — skip. Spread and uncertainty will eat that edge.
  4. Always bet "Half Kelly" or less. The "Full Kelly" amount is the theoretical max — half is safer when you're wrong.
  5. Never risk more than 2% of your bankroll on one market. Even if the math says more.

Important

This tool helps you avoid bad bets. It doesn't predict winners. Polymarket may not be legal in your country. Only bet money you can fully afford to lose.
